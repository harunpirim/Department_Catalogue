Big-Picture Engineering

IMSE at a Glance: Programs, Pathways, and Career Flexibility

Where Big Picture Thinking Takes the Lead. Choosing an engineering path is not always clear-cut. Mechanical, electrical, and civil engineering each play an important role, but IMSE is for students who want to connect the parts and ask, "How can we make this better?"

IMSE is the big-picture discipline for organizers, optimizers, and visionaries. If you see patterns, ask questions, and want to lead change, IMSE turns ideas into practical solutions.

What is Industrial and Manufacturing Systems Engineering?

Industrial and manufacturing systems engineering is about designing, improving, and managing complex systems. IMSE professionals streamline how people, machines, and information work together to make processes more efficient, productive, and people-focused.

  • A mechanical engineer might design the chassis or suspension of an electric car
  • An electrical engineer might focus on battery technology or onboard systems
  • An IMSE professional designs the whole factory and optimizes flow from supply chain to delivery
What does my future look like with an IMSE degree?

An IMSE degree broadens your horizon and helps you create meaningful change. IMSE is not just about factory floors; it is about managing entire operations.

  • NDSU IMSE reports a 96% full-time employment rate
  • Entertainment: design theme park flow and ride queues
  • Healthcare: improve patient wait times and hospital logistics
  • Finance: optimize trading systems and workflows
  • E-commerce and logistics: power smarter global operations
  • Airlines and tech startups: scale scheduling and operations
Is IMSE Right for You?

If you see the big picture and want to improve it, IMSE might be your path forward.

  • Do you enjoy organizing chaos and making things run smoothly?
  • Do you naturally connect the dots while others focus on one piece?
  • Do you like having a plan but can pivot when things change?

These are the instincts of a systems thinker and the foundation of great leadership.
