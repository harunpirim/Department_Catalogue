Department Snapshot

What is Industrial & Manufacturing Systems Engineering?

IMSE focuses on designing, improving, and operating complex systems where people, machines, information, and resources interact. Our students learn to model reality, optimize decisions, and deliver practical, measurable impact for organizations and communities.

Core Themes
  • Optimization & decision analytics
  • Operations research & simulation
  • Human-centered systems design
  • Manufacturing & supply chain
Application Areas
  • Healthcare operations
  • Energy and infrastructure
  • Transportation & logistics
  • AI-enabled decision support
Skills You Build
  • Systems thinking
  • Data storytelling
  • Technical communication
  • Leadership & teamwork
Lab Highlight

Connect Elicit Learn (CELL) Lab

The CELL Lab brings together experts from industrial engineering, computer science, and political science to advance research in mathematical programming, network analysis, machine learning, and computational social science. The lab applies graph theory and AI to real-world challenges ranging from biological networks to power grids. Learn more about the team and current projects on the official lab page: ndsu.edu/labs/cell/we_are.

Research Focus
  • Graph theory and network science
  • Machine learning for prediction
  • Computational social science
  • Decision analytics for complex systems
Why It Matters
  • Turns data into actionable intelligence
  • Improves robustness of critical networks
  • Connects AI with real-world decision making
  • Builds multidisciplinary research teams
Why Industrial Engineering?

Decision Science

Decision Science in IMSE

Decision science uses data, probability, and modeling to identify the best actions in uncertain environments. It blends statistics, operations research, and behavioral insights to help organizations choose wisely.

Methods We Teach
  • Predictive analytics
  • Optimization under uncertainty
  • Simulation and scenario planning
  • Multi-criteria decision making
Where It Applies
  • Healthcare resource planning
  • Smart manufacturing systems
  • Energy grid resilience
  • Public sector policy evaluation
Operations Research + AI

Operations Research in the Modern AI Landscape

AI is powerful, but it needs disciplined decision frameworks. Operations research (OR) provides the optimization, constraints, and explainability needed to deploy AI responsibly. Together they enable systems that are both intelligent and trustworthy.

How OR Elevates AI
  • Optimizes model outputs into real actions
  • Balances trade-offs with constraints
  • Improves fairness and transparency
  • Supports robust, scalable deployment
AI-Driven OR Use Cases
  • Supply chain risk mitigation
  • Smart energy dispatch
  • Transportation routing at scale
  • Automated scheduling systems
Student Advantage
  • Blend optimization with ML
  • Deliver explainable outcomes
  • Build decision intelligence
  • Lead AI-enabled transformation
